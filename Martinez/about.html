Since 1972, Martinez Gallery has been a premiere showcase for graffiti works under the direction of its founder, Hugo Martinez. Over the years, the gallery moved to several New York City neighborhoods. maintaining the same profile, but from 2014, when it opened at his current home on Broadway and 135th Street, West Harlem, the gallery extended its purpose to reflect the social, cultural, and economic realities of the community. Thus, on the one hand, the gallery aimed at redefining how art interacts with people, how it can change people's lives, and how it can avoid the commodification and control of creativity in the "art world." To this end, Martinez Gallery has favored those artists who are engaged in community-related projects, emphasizing links and collaborations with graffiti artists and writers, from New York and from other parts of the world, who are working in related areas.

On the other hand, following the example of Albert Einstein’s observation that “after a certain high level of technical skill is achieved, science and art tend to coalesce in esthetics, plasticity, and form,” the gallery merged with a new clinic for pediatrics and youth support that seeks to address the root causes of major threats to the health of urban families, the so-called millennial morbidities: infant mortality, childhood obesity, academic failure, domestic violence, teen pregnancy, and sexually transmitted diseases. Over this period, Martinez Gallery assembled a team of thought leaders, consisting of scholars, designers, poets, dancers, DJs, architects, doctors, filmmakers, curators, and critics, who, in different ways, at different moments, and under various circumstances, have not only helped to develop this new project but, more important, have focused on ideas and practices embracing the concept of health literacy, and on changing the conversation regarding the relationship between the arts and the sciences.
